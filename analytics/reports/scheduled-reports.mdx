IronBee's scheduled reports let you automatically send a snapshot of any dashboard to one or more recipients on a recurring basis. Reports are delivered as images or PDFs via email, or posted directly to a Slack channel. Rather than asking teammates to log in and check dashboards manually, you can push the data to them on a schedule that fits their workflow. This page covers creating a schedule, configuring delivery options, and managing or pausing existing schedules.

## Before you begin

- You need an **Admin** or **Member** role to create scheduled reports. See [Roles and permissions](/platform/administration/roles-permissions).
- The dashboard you want to report on must already exist. See [Create and share an IronBee dashboard](/analytics/dashboards/create-dashboard).
- To deliver reports to Slack, the IronBee Slack app must be installed in your Slack workspace.

<Note>
Slack delivery requires the IronBee Slack app to be installed in your Slack workspace and authorized to post to the target channel. If you haven't installed it yet, ask a Slack workspace admin to do so via **Settings → Integrations → Slack** in IronBee. The app needs at minimum the `chat:write` permission.
</Note>

## Create a scheduled report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Scheduled Reports">
    In the left sidebar, navigate to **Analytics → Reports**, then click **New scheduled report**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a dashboard">
    Choose the dashboard you want to snapshot from the dropdown. Every chart currently on the dashboard will be included in the report.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the frequency and timing">
    Choose how often the report should run and when:

    | Frequency | Available time settings |
    |---|---|
    | **Daily** | Time of day (hour and minute); time zone. |
    | **Weekly** | Day of week; time of day; time zone. |
    | **Monthly** | Day of month (1–28) or "last day of month"; time of day; time zone. |

    <Info>
    All schedules run in the time zone you select. If recipients are spread across time zones, choose a time that works for the majority or schedule separate reports per region.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose delivery channels and add recipients">
    Select one or both delivery channels:

    **Email**
    - Click **Add email recipient** and enter the email address.
    - Repeat to add multiple recipients.
    - Recipients do not need an IronBee account to receive email reports.

    **Slack**
    - Click **Add Slack channel** and select a channel from the list.
    - Only channels the IronBee Slack app has been invited to will appear.
    - To post to a private channel, first invite the IronBee app to that channel in Slack (`/invite @IronBee`).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose the report format">
    Select the output format for the snapshot:

    - **PDF** — a paginated document with all charts, suitable for email attachments and archiving.
    - **Image (PNG)** — a single-image screenshot of the dashboard, useful for Slack posts and quick sharing.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Name and save the schedule">
    Give the schedule a descriptive name — for example, "Weekly Sales Summary → #sales-team" — then click **Save schedule**. The report will run at the next occurrence of the configured time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Manage existing schedules

Navigate to **Analytics → Reports** to see all scheduled reports in your workspace.

### Edit a schedule

1. Find the schedule in the list and click **Edit** (pencil icon).
2. Update the frequency, recipients, format, or dashboard selection.
3. Click **Save schedule** to apply changes. Changes take effect at the next scheduled run.

### Pause a schedule

If you want to temporarily stop a report from running without deleting it:

1. Find the schedule and click the **...** menu.
2. Select **Pause**. The status changes to **Paused** and no further deliveries occur.
3. To resume, open the same menu and select **Resume**.

### Delete a schedule

1. Find the schedule and click the **...** menu.
2. Select **Delete**, then confirm when prompted.

<Warning>
Deleting a schedule is permanent. Recipients will not receive further reports, and the schedule configuration cannot be recovered. If you only want to stop deliveries temporarily, use **Pause** instead.
</Warning>
